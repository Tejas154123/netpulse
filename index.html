What we measure

Three numbers that
tell the whole story

Most issues with streaming, gaming, and video calls come down to these three metrics. Here's what each one means for you.

Download speed

How fast data travels from the internet to your device. Affects streaming, browsing, loading files, and updates. Most households need 25+ Mbps per person for 4K video.

Ping / latency

Round-trip time in milliseconds. Critical for online gaming and video calls. Under 20ms is excellent, 20–80ms is good, above 150ms causes noticeable lag.

Upload speed

How fast data travels from your device to the internet. Matters for video calls, live streaming, uploading to cloud storage, and sending large files.

Under the hood

How NetPulse tests
your connection

We use real network requests — not estimates — to measure your actual speeds.

STEP 01

Ping measurement

Sends 5 lightweight requests to Cloudflare's edge network and calculates the median round-trip time, filtering out spikes.

STEP 02

Download test

Fetches a 10 MB file from Cloudflare's speed endpoint and precisely measures how long the transfer takes to calculate Mbps.

STEP 03

Upload test

Generates 4 MB of random data in your browser and sends it to a test server, measuring the true outbound throughput.

Get better speeds

Tips to improve your
test results

Getting an unexpectedly slow result? These steps often help before you call your ISP.

Restart your router

Power cycle your router and modem. Unplug for 30 seconds, plug back in. This fixes most temporary slowdowns.

Use a wired connection

Plug directly into the router with an ethernet cable to eliminate Wi-Fi interference. Speeds can improve by 30–50%.

Close background apps

Close other browser tabs, pause updates, and shut any apps syncing to the cloud before running the test.

Test at different times

ISP congestion happens during peak hours (7–11 PM). Test in the morning to see your true baseline speed.

Disconnect other devices

Ask others on your network to pause streaming or downloads during the test for the most accurate reading.

Call your ISP

If speeds are consistently below your plan's advertised rate, contact your ISP — they may have a line issue or can upgrade your plan.
